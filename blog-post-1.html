The Best Eco-Friendly Cleaning Products for Hawaii Homes

Home › Blog › Eco-Friendly Products
By Leilani Kahananui June 10, 2025 5 min read

Why Eco-Friendly Cleaning Matters in Hawaii

Hawaii is home to some of the world's most delicate ecosystems — coral reefs, native rainforests, and freshwater streams that are home to endemic species found nowhere else on Earth. Every product we pour down the drain eventually finds its way into these ecosystems. Conventional cleaning chemicals containing phosphates, synthetic fragrances, and chlorine bleach are particularly damaging to coral reefs and marine life.

At Nalu Clean, we made the switch to fully eco-friendly products from day one — not as a marketing gimmick, but because we love this island and take our responsibility to protect it seriously. Here's what we've learned after cleaning hundreds of Maui homes with plant-based alternatives.

Our Top Eco-Friendly Product Picks

1. Branch Basics Concentrate

Branch Basics is a plant-derived, fragrance-free cleaning concentrate that you dilute for different purposes — all-purpose spray, bathroom cleaner, laundry, you name it. One bottle replaces your entire cleaning cabinet. It's certified non-toxic and completely biodegradable. We use it on countertops, appliances, and floors throughout every home we clean.

2. Better Life Natural All-Purpose Cleaner

This Hawaiian Islands favorite is made from plant-derived surfactants and comes in naturally scented options like clary sage and citrus. It cuts through grease and grime without leaving harsh chemical residues. The clary sage scent is a particular favorite with our clients who want that "clean without chemicals" smell.

3. Seventh Generation Disinfecting Multi-Surface Cleaner

When true disinfection is needed — for vacation rental turnovers or bathroom sanitization — Seventh Generation's thymol-based disinfectant meets EPA standards without chlorine bleach. Thymol is derived from thyme oil and is highly effective against bacteria and viruses.

4. Mrs. Meyer's Clean Day

A local favorite available at Safeway and Foodland throughout Maui, Mrs. Meyer's uses essential oils and plant-derived ingredients. Their lavender and lemon verbena scents are particularly popular for bathrooms and kitchens.

5. DIY Vinegar + Water Spray

Sometimes the best cleaner is also the simplest. A 50/50 solution of white distilled vinegar and water cleans glass, mirrors, and stainless steel surfaces beautifully. It's non-toxic, antibacterial, and costs almost nothing. Note: don't use vinegar on natural stone like granite or marble.

What to Avoid in Hawaii

Certain conventional products are particularly harmful in Hawaii's environment and should be replaced whenever possible:

  • Bleach (sodium hypochlorite): Toxic to aquatic life and often unnecessary when plant-based disinfectants work just as well.
  • Ammonia-based cleaners: Creates hazardous fumes in enclosed spaces and is harmful to marine ecosystems.
  • Synthetic fragrances: Often contain phthalates, which are endocrine disruptors — especially concerning for keiki and pets.
  • Single-use plastic packaging: Choose concentrates in refillable containers to reduce plastic waste.

Making the Switch

Switching to eco-friendly cleaning products doesn't have to happen all at once. Start by replacing the products you use most frequently — your all-purpose spray and dish soap. As you run out of conventional products, replace them with plant-based alternatives. Within a few months, you'll have a completely non-toxic cleaning cabinet.
