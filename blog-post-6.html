Green Cleaning Tips for Families with Kids and Pets in Hawaii

Home › Blog › Family Green Cleaning
By Leilani Kahananui April 5, 2025 4 min read

The Stakes Are Higher With Keiki and Pets

Babies and toddlers spend an enormous amount of time on the floor. They put their hands in their mouths constantly. Young children are more vulnerable to chemical exposure than adults because their bodies are still developing, their immune systems aren't fully formed, and they breathe air at floor level where chemical residues linger longest after conventional cleaning.

Pets face similar risks. Dogs and cats groom themselves constantly, licking paws that have walked across just-cleaned floors. If that floor was cleaned with a chlorine-based product or quaternary ammonium disinfectant, those chemicals end up in their system.

In Hawaii, where we're already so conscious of what goes into our water and reef systems, it makes perfect sense to extend that care to what we put on our home surfaces too.

Safe Swaps for Common Cleaning Products

Floor Cleaner

Most conventional floor cleaners leave a chemical film that pets and crawling babies are directly exposed to. Swap to: diluted castile soap (Dr. Bronner's) in warm water, or Branch Basics diluted to their "floor" dilution. Both clean effectively and leave no harmful residues after drying.

Bathroom Disinfectant

Instead of bleach, use hydrogen peroxide (3%) as a disinfectant spray. It kills bacteria and viruses effectively, then breaks down into water and oxygen — completely non-toxic. Spray on, let sit for 5 minutes, wipe off. Add a few drops of tea tree oil to enhance antibacterial power.

All-Purpose Spray

Make your own: combine 1 cup water, 1 cup white vinegar, and 15 drops of lavender or lemon essential oil in a spray bottle. Effective on most surfaces and 100% safe for keiki and pets. Note: not suitable for granite, marble, or natural stone surfaces.

Carpet and Upholstery

For odor and light staining, baking soda is your friend. Sprinkle generously, let sit for 20–30 minutes, then vacuum up. For pet accidents, use an enzyme cleaner — these are specifically designed to break down pet proteins that cause odors and stains.

Baby and Pet-Specific Cleaning Tips

  • Wash baby toys weekly with diluted castile soap. Rinse thoroughly and air dry in the sun — Hawaii sunlight is a natural sanitizer.
  • Clean pet food bowls daily — in Hawaii's heat and humidity, bacteria grow quickly in food residue.
  • After any cleaning session, ventilate the house for at least 30 minutes before allowing kids and pets back in, even with non-toxic products.
  • Store all cleaning products in locked cabinets at all times — even "natural" products can be harmful in large quantities.
  • Wash pet bedding weekly in a hot cycle with an unscented, dye-free detergent.
