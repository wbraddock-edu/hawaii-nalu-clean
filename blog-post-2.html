The Ultimate Vacation Rental Cleaning Checklist for Maui Hosts

Home › Blog › Vacation Rental Checklist
By Kaimana Kahananui May 28, 2025 7 min read

Why Your Turnover Clean Makes or Breaks Your Reviews

In today's vacation rental landscape, guests have near-infinite options. A single bad review mentioning dirt, hair in the drain, or a mildewy smell can tank your booking rate for months. In Maui's competitive rental market — where guests are paying $300–$1,000+ per night — the cleanliness bar is sky high.

After hundreds of vacation rental turnovers on Maui, we've developed this comprehensive checklist that ensures nothing gets missed, from the broom closet to the lanai. Use it yourself or share it with your cleaning team.

Before You Start: Prep Work

  • Check the checkout time — give yourself at least 3–4 hours before next check-in
  • Review guest notes — any damage reported? Extra messes?
  • Do a quick walkthrough to assess scope before deep cleaning
  • Strip all linens immediately and start laundry

Kitchen Checklist

  • Wash all dishes and check dishwasher (run if needed)
  • Wipe exterior of all appliances — microwave, fridge, stove, toaster
  • Clean inside microwave (this is the #1 thing guests check)
  • Wipe stovetop burners and drip pans
  • Clean inside fridge — remove all guest food, wipe shelves
  • Wipe cabinet fronts and handles
  • Clean sink and faucet — polish to shine
  • Sweep and mop floor
  • Restock: coffee, cooking oil, dish soap, paper towels, trash bags

Bathroom Checklist

  • Clean toilet bowl, seat, exterior — sanitize thoroughly
  • Scrub shower and tub — remove all hair (critical!)
  • Clean grout lines — Maui's humidity causes buildup fast
  • Polish mirror and sink faucet
  • Wipe vanity and clean inside drawers
  • Mop floor and clean behind toilet
  • Replace toilet paper roll — fold end into hotel point
  • Restock: shampoo, conditioner, body wash, hand soap, fresh towels

Bedroom Checklist

  • Strip and remake all beds with fresh linens
  • Inspect mattress protectors — wash if needed
  • Fluff and arrange decorative pillows
  • Check under beds for forgotten items
  • Dust all surfaces, lamps, and nightstands
  • Wipe mirrors and glass surfaces
  • Vacuum carpet or sweep and mop floors
  • Check closets — clear any guest items

Living Areas and Lanai

  • Dust all surfaces, TV, and entertainment console
  • Vacuum sofas and throw pillows
  • Wipe down glass tables and windows
  • Check and clean ceiling fans — common in Hawaii
  • Sweep and clean lanai — Maui gets red dirt and pollen
  • Clean outdoor furniture
  • Empty all trash cans and replace liners

Final Walkthrough: The Guest Eye Test

Before you leave, do a final walkthrough at guest height. Look for: smudges on mirrors, crumbs on counters, hair anywhere, items out of place. Take photos of each room for your records — this protects you in case of damage disputes.

Pro tip: light a lemon or citrus-scented candle 20 minutes before guest check-in. That first impression when they walk through the door is everything.
