How to Prepare Your Home for Maui's Trade Wind Season

Home › Blog › Trade Wind Season
By Kaimana Kahananui April 18, 2025 5 min read

What Are Trade Winds and When Do They Peak?

Maui's trade winds — the gentle, consistent northeast breezes that keep the island comfortable — are most reliable and strongest from May through September. These winds are why so much of Maui stays so pleasant even when temperatures rise. But they also carry fine volcanic dust, pollen from windward plants, and salt particles from the ocean.

For homeowners on Maui, trade wind season means a few things: your house will be dustier than usual, pollen counts on the windward side can be significant for allergy sufferers, and anything left outdoors will get extra exposure to salt-laden air.

Pre-Season Prep: May Checklist

Screens and Windows

Before trade winds pick up, clean all window screens with a soft brush and mild soap solution. Remove built-up dust from the previous winter so it doesn't blow into your home when you open windows. While you're at it, clean the window tracks — these collect an astonishing amount of grime and can prevent windows from sliding properly.

Air Conditioning Filters

Replace or clean your A/C filters before trade wind season starts. Fine volcanic dust and pollen will clog filters faster during this period. We recommend checking filters monthly from May through October.

Outdoor Furniture

Wipe down all outdoor furniture and apply a UV protectant to plastics and ratan. Salt air during trade wind season can accelerate oxidation on metal furniture. Consider covers for pieces you don't use frequently.

During Trade Wind Season: Keep Up With These

  • Weekly dust: Surfaces accumulate faster. Focus on fans, window sills, and electronics.
  • Doorway mats: Keep quality mats at all entry points — they trap volcanic red dirt and pollen before it enters the house.
  • Allergy season mode: Keep windows on the windward side (generally northeast-facing) closed during peak pollen hours (early morning). Open leeward-side windows to keep airflow without pollen.
  • Rinse outdoor spaces weekly: Lanais, walkways, and outdoor kitchens benefit from a weekly rinse to remove salt and dust buildup.

End-of-Season Deep Clean: October

After trade wind season, schedule a thorough deep clean. Focus on:

  • Deep cleaning A/C units and replacing filters
  • Detailed dusting of all furniture, fans, and light fixtures
  • Cleaning window screens and tracks
  • Cleaning and conditioning outdoor furniture
  • Vacuuming upholstery and area rugs
